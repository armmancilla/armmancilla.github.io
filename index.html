PROJECTS

Random Walks in Annihilation Dynamics

The annihilation is the process of two particles colliding and disappearing. We study the concentration decay of particles undegoing this dynamic.

The concentration decay might or might not approach zero, but the certainty of our model? Asymptotically undeniable.

Computational Simulation of Thermodynamic Systems

We simulate gas particles using Monte-Carlo and Metropolis-Hastings. Designed as an educational tool for understanding properties like pressure and temperature in non-ideal systems.

Metropolis-Hastings? More like Metropolis-FAST-ings with these convergence rates.

Statistics of the Random Walk with Resetting and Self-Memory

Random walks involve taking random steps, where resetting can return the walker to the origin, and self-memory allows returning to a previous position. This study presents a statistical analysis of the effects of these dynamics.

Resetting sends walkers home—unlike these results, which are going places

Epidemic Models for Heard Immunity & Social Distancing

We implemented epidemic models to infected waves in Mexico during the COVID-19 pandemic. We derived analytical solutions where possible and linked findings to the Susceptible-Infected-Removed (SIR) model.

Turns out, 'stay apart' is the only relationship advice epidemiologists agree on
